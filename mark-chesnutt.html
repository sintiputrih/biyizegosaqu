When was Mark Chesnutt born?

On September 6, 1963, Mark Nelson Chesnutt was born in Beaumont, Texas. He was strongly influenced by his father, musician and record collector Bob Chesnutt, who also took him to start recording when he was 17 years old. As a young youngster, Mark picked up the drums after receiving a drum set for Christmas.

In his eleventh grade year, Mark quit high school and started playing in bars in Texas. He was transported to Nashville, Tennessee, by his father when he was 17 years old to start recording his music. He recorded his music with numerous regional labels and eventually became the house band for the Beaumont nightclub Cutters.

Career

With the help of his first album, Doing My Country Thing, which he was able to release in 1988, Mark’s career began when he was still in his teens. His major-label debut, “Too Cold at Home,” came out in 1990. The album has five chart singles on the Billboard Hot Country Singles & Tracks chart.

“Brother Jukebox,” his first number-one single on Hot Country Songs, was released in 1991. Many people praised his voice, and the record earned positive reviews. In 1994, after selling over a million copies, it received a platinum certification.

Mark’s second album, Longneck & Short Stories, was released in 1992. Additionally, it was given a platinum certification, and the song “I’ll Think of Something” topped the Billboard charts. His last album to be distributed by MCA, Almost Goodbye, came out in 1993.
