    Mori Career

    In 1992, Mori began her career as a fashion model at the age of 14, later she became an actress when she co-starred in 1997 on the smash TV hit Mirada De Mujer with TV Azteca,  then, she was featured in the soap opera Azul Tequila. She has also made an appearance on several lists as one of the most gorgeous Mexican actresses of all time.

    Mori got her first leading role as “Azul” in the series Azul Tequila in 1998, co-starring with Mauricio Ochmann. A year later she filmed the series Me muero por tí in Miami, alongside Peruvian actor Christian Meier. She later acted in several other telenovelas, such as the Telemundo telenovela Amor descarado.

    Mori got her first film role in the romantic comedy Inspiración in 2001 the film was a box-office success in cinemas and in 2002, she played the villain role as a seductive college student in the telenovela Subete A Mi Moto opposite Vanessa Acosta. Mori signs with Televisa in 2004 and starred as Rubí, a smash TV-hit and highly–rated Mexican soap opera, of the same name, and as result, she acquired another TVyNovelas Award

    She appeared in the psychological crime thriller Amor, Dolor y Viceversa alongside Leonardo Sbaraglia in 2009. The drama is about a female architect who is involved in the murder of the fiancee of a promising surgeon, who allegedly saw in her nightmares. The crime drama was filmed in Mexico City and is produced by Mori. The film received mixed reviews from critics and viewers.
